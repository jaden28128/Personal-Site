Professional Experience

Currently, I serve as an Undergraduate Researcher at Virginia Tech’s Department of Computer Science under Dr. Naren Ramakrishnan, where I’m building a mathematics problem-generating and solving LLM system using modern APIs and frameworks. Previously, at George Mason University, I worked as a Student Researcher under Professor Ales Psaker. There, I applied machine learning to model and visualize quantum mechanics and chaotic physical systems, successfully predicting chaotic function patterns up to two minutes in advance with 45% accuracy. I’ve also interned at Inspirit AI, where I developed a sentiment-based financial prediction model with 84% accuracy on NASDAQ and Forbes 500 stock trends, gaining hands-on experience with LLMs, BERT, transformers, and neural networks.

As a Programmer for First Robotics Team 2068, I developed backend software for autonomous robotics functions using Java and computer vision tools like PhotonVision. We placed in the top 17% of global teams and advanced to the 2024 FRC World Championship. Additionally, I led the design and machine learning development of Amplytics, a React and Flask web app predicting U.S. city electricity costs based on scraped and cleaned datasets. These experiences have not only strengthened my full-stack and AI programming skills but also shaped me into a well-rounded, detail-oriented problem solver committed to impactful innovation.
